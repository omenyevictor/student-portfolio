About Us

Cyber security student, web developer, and lifelong learner — here's a closer look at my background, goals, and the skills I bring to every project.

I'm Victor Omenye, a cyber security student and web developer with a passion for building digital experiences that are both secure and genuinely easy to use. This page is a closer look at where I've come from, where I'm headed, and what I bring to the table — my education, my goals, my technical toolkit, and the things I enjoy outside of the screen.

Educational Background

My academic path has been shaped by a steady pull toward both security and the web. I'm currently pursuing coursework in cyber security and software engineering, building a solid foundation in network defense, ethical hacking, cryptography, and secure application design alongside formal training in web development.

Hands-on labs — from vulnerability assessments to building and breaking small web applications — have shaped how I think about problems: not just "does it work," but "how could this fail, and for whom." That mindset now runs through everything I build.

Career Aspirations

My goal is to build a career at the intersection of cyber security and web development — helping teams ship products that are both delightful to use and resistant to attack. Too often those two goals are treated as trade-offs; I want to prove they don't have to be.

In the next few years, I'm aiming to specialize in application security, contribute to open-source security tooling, and eventually mentor other developers making the same transition into security-conscious engineering. Starting a cyber security podcast has been part of that journey — a way to think out loud about the field and share what I'm learning as I go.

Hobbies and Interests

Outside of security research and code, I stay curious in other ways — following emerging tech and security news, tinkering with open-source projects, and playing strategy games that scratch the same problem-solving itch as security work.

I'm also an avid podcast listener, which is part of what led me to start recording one myself — the cyber security show featured on this site. When I need to properly switch off, you'll usually find me outdoors or catching up on a good book.
